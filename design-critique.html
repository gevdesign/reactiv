01 · Color

Good instinct, half finished

The bright green on black is the most distinctive choice on the site. It’s the right instinct — it just hasn’t been carried through strongly enough to shape the full brand experience.

The green is the signature. Right now it feels like a button color, not a brand system.

What works

Bright green on black is immediately distinctive.

The dark background feels premium and serious.

The overall instinct is more memorable than another blue-purple tech palette.

What’s not working

The green only appears in a couple of places, mostly the button.

A muted secondary green adds noise without contributing much.

The page ends up flatter than it should because the strongest color is underused.

Use the green on key numbers, section dividers, icons, and emphasis moments so it feels like brand language — not just CTA styling.

The type is fine. It just doesn’t create enough contrast to leave a mark.

What works

Text sizing works well from mobile to desktop.

The current type doesn’t fight with the product message.

The tone feels clean and modern enough for enterprise.

What’s not working

There’s very little contrast between the two fonts.

The stats don’t hit hard enough visually.

The headline is readable, but it doesn’t leave a strong impression.

The strongest sales assets on the page are the numbers. They should feel like headlines, not supporting copy.
03 · Imagery

Shows the demo, loses the story

4

The current imagery shows what an App Clip can look like, but it doesn’t build confidence that Reactiv operates at the scale the copy is promising.

The screenshot explains the product. It doesn’t explain why a serious buyer should trust it.

What works

The product is visible and easy to understand at a glance.

The layout follows a familiar SaaS product-story pattern.

What’s not working

The fashion promo screenshot feels too small-brand and template-like.

There’s no real-world retail context, scale, or environment.

The visuals don’t reinforce the enterprise-level claims in the copy.

Lead with an image that communicates trust, scale, or real-world context. Show the product second, not first.
04 · Brand Fit

The words say big league, the design says not yet

6

There’s a clear gap between what the copy promises and what the visuals communicate. The site doesn’t need a new direction — it needs the current direction executed more convincingly.

The brand direction is right. The execution just hasn’t caught up to the ambition yet.

What works

The logo is sharp and memorable.

The dark visual direction is right for the audience.

The testimonials and metrics carry real weight.

What’s not working

The visuals feel more startup-template than enterprise-infrastructure.

The brand color isn’t doing enough brand work.

Without the logo, the site doesn’t feel distinct enough yet.

This is why the site feels close but not finished: the core instinct is right, but the details aren’t reinforcing the same level of confidence.

Recommended changes

Four specific fixes, not a full redesign

These changes would close most of the gap while keeping the current direction intact.

Fix 1

Make the green do real work

Thread it through key numbers, dividers, icons, and emphasis moments so it becomes part of the system.

Fix 2

Make the stats hit harder

The biggest numbers on the page should feel visually heavier and more persuasive than the surrounding copy.

Fix 3

Replace the hero visual

Use imagery that signals scale, trust, and real-world retail context instead of a template-style promo screenshot.

Fix 4

Break the scroll rhythm

Introduce one or two lighter sections so the page feels paced and intentional rather than visually monotone.
